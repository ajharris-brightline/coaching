Understanding Behavioral Health Coaching

Coaching is a credentialed, evidence-based practice

Brightline coaches operate within a rigorous professional framework. NBC-HWC (National Board for Health & Wellness Coaching) and ICF standards shape how goals are set, how progress is tracked, and how coaches facilitate change. Our system must honor — not override — these competencies.

🤝
Collaborative Goal Setting
Per NBHWC standards, goals are co-created with the client and grounded in their values and readiness. IGAR goals are collaborative — set with family in Session 1, not prescribed. The system supports this by making goals visible and editable.
🧭
Client-Centered, Self-Directed Change
Coaching is not therapy — it's a partnership. The ICF defines coaching as "partnering with clients in a thought-provoking and creative process." Skill Cards must support coach facilitation, not replace clinical judgment or mandate prescribed sequences.
📈
Motivational Interviewing & Behavior Change
NBC-HWC competencies center MI, SDT (Self-Determination Theory), and positive psychology. Coaches build self-efficacy. The IGAR 1–7 scale tracks goal movement collaboratively each session — not clinician-rated, but family-reported progress.
🔬
Measurement-Based Practice
Research shows that tracking outcomes improves them. Currently we have no way to connect what interventions are being used with what outcomes are achieved. This initiative creates that data layer for the first time — informing coaching quality at scale.
📋
Documentation in Healthie
Coaches currently use two note types (Welcome Session + Ongoing) and assign exercises and handouts through the platform. Post-session summaries exist but aren't standard practice. The new system integrates all of this into a single, purposeful flow.
🧩
Scope of Practice
Coaching is distinct from therapy. NBHWC scope does not include diagnosis or clinical treatment. Our system maintains this distinction — coaching Skill Cards draw from the GEBI (Glossary of Evidence-Based Interventions) within coaching scope, separate from clinical treatment plans.

How Skill Cards Work

Built like Legos.
Grounded in evidence.

Each Skill Card represents one core intervention drawn from the GEBI — CBT, ACT, DBT, MATCH/PMT, or PCIT. Some naturally overlap (the Cognitive Triangle informs almost everything), and that's by design.

Coaches select and sequence cards for each member. AI suggests based on presenting concerns and assessment data. The coach always makes the final call before anything reaches the family.

A foundation card like Cognitive Triangle gets introduced early and stays active throughout — new cards layer on top as the member builds skills. This mirrors how expert coaches actually work.

Care Support modules (potty training, picky eating) are supplemental cards that sit alongside any pillar — not standalone programs.

The AI Ecosystem

AI that supports the coach.
Never replaces them.

The AI layer in this system is an intelligent assistant — it surfaces information, drafts content, and tracks patterns. But the coach is always the decision-maker. This is the principle: AI suggests, human decides.

🌅
Pre-Session Summary
Before each session, AI reads intake data, assessment scores, prior notes, and goal progress to generate a concise briefing — clinical flags, suggested focus areas, and what changed since last time. Saves coaches 15+ minutes of prep.
🧩
Care Plan Suggestions
After the Welcome Session, AI suggests a starting care plan based on presenting concerns, PSC-17 subscales, and the family's stated goals. The coach reviews, modifies, and approves. Nothing reaches the member without human sign-off.
🌇
Post-Session Summary
After each session, AI drafts a summary of what was covered, what was assigned, and what the plan is for next session — pulling from session notes and care plan updates. Coach edits and saves. Optional, but opt-in encouraged.
📈
Pattern Recognition
Over time, the system tracks which interventions correlate with goal progress for which presenting concerns. This generates insights for QA, supervision, and program development — the research layer we've never had before.
🎯
Goal Tracking & IGAR
IGAR scores are logged weekly per goal. AI visualizes trajectory and flags when a goal is stalling. The 1–7 scale stays collaborative — coach and family update together each session.
👨‍👩‍👧
Family-Facing Translation
Clinical care plan language gets automatically translated into simple, warm language for the family portal. "Exposure & Desensitization" becomes "Facing fears one step at a time." Progress charts show in plain English.
